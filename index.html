Shaifali Parashar

I am a CNRS Research scientist at LIRIS, INSA-Lyon. Previously, I was a postdoc researcher at CVLAB, EPFL working on 3D computer vision with Prof. Pascal Fua. I received my PhD in Computer Vision at University of Clermont-Auvergne, France. My PhD thesis received the award for best thesis. I was supervised by Prof. Adrien Bartoli and Dr. Daniel Pizarro.

I work on research topics related to 3D Computer Vision with strong interests in 3D reconstruction of deformable objects from RGB cameras and event-based cameras. Recently, I have also gained interest in 3D modeling of deformable objects with application to garment draping and ultrasound simulations.

I review for major computer vision and machine learning conferences (CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, NeurIPS, ICLR, ICML, AAAI, ACCV, 3DV, WACV) and journals (PAMI, IJCV), robotics conference (IROS) and journal (I-RAL) and computer graphics conferences (SIGGRAPH) and journals (TVCG, CGF).

To apply for masters internships, please send me an email with CV and transcripts. I am always looking for good students. For a prospective PhD and postdoc, please send an inquiry via email along with CV and two letters of recommendations.
